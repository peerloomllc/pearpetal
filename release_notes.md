This release is about staying connected. PearPetal can now reach your other devices, and your partner's, even on mobile networks that will not let two phones talk to each other directly.

New

  - Connect anywhere. Some mobile networks will not let two phones connect straight to each other, which could leave pairing or syncing hanging forever with no explanation. PearPetal can now pass the connection through a PeerLoom helper server, but only after a direct connection has already failed. Your cycle data stays scrambled the whole way and the helper cannot read any of it. It can only see that two devices are talking and how much data passes between them. This is on by default, and you can switch it off under Settings, Connect anywhere, which keeps you strictly device to device.
  - Connection details. Under Connect anywhere you can now see how your phone is really connecting: how many connections it made directly and whether the helper was ever needed.

Improved

  - A tidier Settings screen. Settings used to be several screens of scrolling with no clear order, and some things sat permanently open while more important ones were hidden. Everything is now grouped under Your cycle, How it looks, Alerts and connection, then Your data, and each group opens when you tap it. Your name and photo stay at the top.

Fixed

  - On smaller phones, the Dial and Month switcher no longer covers the flower button and the information button at the top of the cycle card.

Everything you track still stays on your own devices.
